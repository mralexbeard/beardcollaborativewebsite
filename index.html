Our Expertise

Applied Improvisation

Improvisation, commonly known as improv, is the art of spontaneously creating something—whether it involves words, physical movements, or more—from scratch. This unscripted process unfolds live in the moment, drawing upon the power of active listening, body language recognition, agreement, and bold decision-making. Ongoing research and past studies affirm that applied improvisation—employing improv principles, tools, practices, skills, and mindsets in non-theatrical settings—not only boosts confidence and self-esteem but also ignites resilience and fosters a sense of belonging.

How We Utilize It

Design Thinking

Design thinking embodies a non-linear iterative process that can be applied to understand the needs and perspectives of people, reframe problems, question assumptions, and devise innovative solutions. Human-centered design concentrates on creating solutions with exceptional usability and an optimal user experience. Both methodologies are valuable when addressing ambiguous or unknown problems and have been proven to enhance psychological empowerment, facilitate knowledge transfer, and foster better collaboration.

About Alex Beard, Founder Beard Collaborative

Alex Beard, the founder of Beard Collaborative, is a graduate of Virginia Tech, where he studied Theatre Arts and Leadership and Social Change. Specializing in public dialogue, design thinking methodologies, applied improvisation, and utilizing the arts as tools for social change, he has built over 15 years of professional experience as an actor, director, teaching artist, and facilitator.

His craft has been refined at prestigious institutions, including the Kennedy Center, Second City, iO, UCB, WIT, and ArscomicA. Alex has propelled creativity and innovation to the forefront of the way we work and problem-solving, leaving an indelible mark on numerous organizations around the country.

He has collaborated and worked with top businesses and institutions such as the Federal Reserve, the Walt Disney Company, USO, Ronald McDonald House Charities, universities, medical systems, K-12 school districts, municipal governments, and many more nationwide.
